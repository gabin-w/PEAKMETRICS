Market Context

PeakMetrics operates at the intersection of the sports nutrition ($61 billion) and HealthTech markets. In 2026, the dominant macro-trend is "Data-Driven Wellness" — consumers demand solutions grounded in biological evidence, not generic formulas. The rise of connected wearables creates a structural window for real-time adaptive nutrition.

02

The Unique Opportunity

The core problem is the "blind supplement". 90% of amateur athletes consume supplements biologically mismatched to their needs, leading to ineffectiveness or overdose. Fear of contamination with banned substances holds back serious athletes entirely. PeakMetrics eliminates guesswork with biologically validated, anti-doping certified formulas.

Enabling every athlete to reach their full potential through precision, biologically personalized and scalable nutrition, rooted in scientific rigor and full transparency.

Marketing Plan

REACHING OUR CUSTOMERS

Our go-to-market strategy targets two core segments: serious amateur athletes (25–45, data-conscious, wearable adopters) and competitive sports professionals requiring certified, safe supplementation.

Target Segments

Segment A — Quantified Amateur: Age 25–45, wearable user, spends €60–120/month on nutrition. Motivated by performance data and health optimization.

Segment B — Certified Athlete: Competes at regional or national level, requires anti-doping compliance, willing to pay a premium for certified, trustworthy products.

Pricing Strategy

Premium subscription at €89/month (starter) to €149/month (full biomarker + wearable integration). Positioned above mass market and below bespoke clinical services — capturing data-driven consumers' willingness to pay.

Legal Considerations

REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

Operating at the intersection of health data law, food supplement regulation, and sports anti-doping rules means legal compliance is a core competency — not an afterthought.

GDPR & Health Data (HDS)
Biomarker data (blood, urine) is classified as sensitive health data under GDPR. PeakMetrics operates on HDS-certified cloud infrastructure with a Data Protection Officer and explicit consent architecture from onboarding.
EU Food Supplement Law (2002/46/EC)
All formulas comply with European directives governing permissible nutrients, maximum dosages, and labelling requirements. Reviewed by a qualified nutritionist before any commercial release.
Anti-Doping Compliance (WADA / Informed Sport)
Products undergo Informed Sport certification — the globally recognized mark for products tested against WADA's Prohibited List — protecting both customers and PeakMetrics from liability in competitive contexts.
Corporate Structure (SAS)
PeakMetrics will incorporate as an SAS (Société par Actions Simplifiée) in France — providing flexibility for equity issuance, investor entry, and future EU single market expansion.
